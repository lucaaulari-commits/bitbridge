How BitBridge Works

1

Partnership Formation

Crypto exchanges join BitBridge federation and deposit collateral (BTC) into common pool. Each exchange maintains an account balance in BitBridge's centralized database.

2

Instant Off-Chain Transfer

When Exchange A needs to send BTC to Exchange B, BitBridge updates balances in its database instantly (like PayPal). Exchange A balance decreases, Exchange B increases. No blockchain transaction!

3

Transaction Recording

All transfers throughout the day are recorded in BitBridge's centralized ledger. Each exchange can see real-time balance updates and transaction history.

4

Netting & Compensation

At end of day (EOD), BitBridge calculates net positions. If Exchange A sent 50 BTC and received 45 BTC, net position = -5 BTC. Only the difference needs on-chain settlement.

5

Daily On-Chain Settlement

Once per day, BitBridge executes on-chain Bitcoin transactions to settle net positions. This ensures security while keeping costs minimal. 1000 transfers ‚Üí 1 on-chain transaction.

6

Collateral Management

Exchanges with negative balances are covered by the common pool. Multi-signature wallets and smart contracts ensure security. BitBridge monitors credit limits and liquidity.
